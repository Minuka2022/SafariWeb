Bundala National Park: A Birdwatcher’s Paradise in Sri Lanka

Bundala National Park is a birdwatcher’s paradise nestled in the southern region of Sri Lanka. This park, recognized as a biosphere reserve by UNESCO, is home to an impressive array of avian species, making it a must-visit destination for bird enthusiasts. The park boasts over 200 species of birds, including both endemic and migratory species. From the vibrant flamingos that flock to the park’s lagoons to the elusive Sri Lanka Junglefowl, Bundala offers a birdwatching experience like no other. The park’s diverse habitats, ranging from forests to wetlands, provide the perfect backdrop for this avian spectacle. But the allure of Bundala extends beyond its bird population. The park is also home to a variety of other wildlife, including elephants, leopards, and crocodiles. As you explore the park, you’ll have the chance to witness these creatures in their natural habitat, adding to the richness of your visit.
